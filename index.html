SENI

Motion System Evaluation & Rotation Axis Intelligence Layer

A system-level framework enabling consistency analysis between estimated motion states and real-world vehicle dynamics for validation of ADAS and mobility systems.

Problem

Modern mobility systems rely on model-based estimation of vehicle motion states across a system-level rotation axis. However, real-world conditions introduce persistent discrepancies between estimated and actual motion behavior.

  • Non-linear tire-road interaction under transient conditions
  • Dynamic load transfer and chassis response variation
  • Coupling between longitudinal, lateral, and yaw motion
  • Model simplification in control and estimation systems
Concept
SENI introduces a motion consistency evaluation layer that quantifies divergence between estimated motion states and observed physical vehicle dynamics, without requiring modification of existing control architectures.
Method
  • System-level evaluation independent of control logic
  • Compatibility with existing estimation frameworks
  • Focus on rotational motion consistency (yaw / axis-level dynamics)
Intended Applications
  • ADAS validation and verification
  • Vehicle dynamics benchmarking
  • Estimation model performance evaluation
  • Simulation vs real-world consistency analysis
Value Proposition
  • Improved observability of system-level motion behavior
  • Structured validation of estimation accuracy
  • Reduced uncertainty in mobility system testing
  • Enhanced interpretability of dynamic vehicle responses
Physical Mapping (Optional)

The framework can be mapped to wheel-level motion behavior for empirical validation and experimental benchmarking.

About
