SENI

Wheel-Level Motion Intelligence for Mobility System Evaluation and Validation

Purpose

This document provides a high-level overview of SENI as a motion intelligence layer intended for evaluation in vehicle dynamics, ADAS validation, and autonomous mobility development environments.

System Context

Current mobility systems rely on indirect estimation models for vehicle and wheel dynamics. In real-world operating conditions, these estimations may diverge from actual motion behavior under non-ideal traction, load variation, and transient dynamics.

SENI Overview
SENI is a wheel-level motion intelligence concept designed to provide additional motion-related observability for system evaluation and validation purposes.
Intended Use in Engineering Workflows
  • Validation of vehicle dynamics and motion estimation models
  • Support for ADAS and autonomous system testing environments
  • Enhancement of system-level motion observability
  • Benchmarking of real-world vs. estimated motion behavior
Integration Considerations
  • Designed as a non-intrusive augmentation layer
  • Compatible with existing vehicle and chassis development architectures
  • No modification required to core vehicle control systems (conceptual design)
Value for Tier-1/OEM Evaluation
  • Improved validation fidelity for motion-related systems
  • Enhanced understanding of real-world dynamic discrepancies
  • Potential reduction of uncertainty in model-based control validation
Status

Concept-stage technology for evaluation and discussion purposes. Not a production system.
