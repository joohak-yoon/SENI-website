SENI

Rotational Motion Intelligence for Industrial and Mobility System Validation
A framework for analyzing consistency between expected and observed motion behavior across industrial rotating systems and mobility applications.

Purpose

SENI is a structured evaluation framework for analyzing rotational motion behavior in modern engineered systems.

It supports system-level understanding of how motion behavior is represented and evaluated across industrial and mobility development environments.

Why It Matters

Validation of complex systems often relies on isolated testing of components, sensors, or estimation models.

However, inconsistencies between expected motion behavior and observed behavior are not always explicitly evaluated at the system level.

SENI addresses this gap through a structured evaluation perspective.

System Context

Modern engineered systems rely on the representation and interpretation of rotational motion behavior under real-world operating conditions.

Differences may exist between expected and observed behavior due to dynamic environmental factors, operational variability, or system interactions.

SENI provides a structured perspective for analyzing these differences at a system abstraction level.

While initially motivated by mobility validation challenges, SENI is designed to support consistency analysis across industrial rotating assets, with vehicle wheel systems representing one application domain.

Concept Overview

SENI defines a structured approach for:
  • Analyzing rotational motion behavior at a system abstraction level
  • Evaluating consistency between different representations of motion behavior
  • Supporting validation-oriented analysis in development workflows
  • Comparative evaluation under real-world operating conditions

Intended Use

Industrial Domain (Primary Focus)

  • Industrial rotating systems
  • Energy and power generation systems
  • Heavy machinery and industrial equipment
  • Rotating equipment condition and behavior validation

Mobility Domain (Secondary Focus)

  • Vehicle dynamics evaluation studies
  • ADAS and autonomous mobility development workflows
  • Mobility system benchmarking and validation studies
  • Motion behavior consistency research

Key Characteristics

  • System-level evaluation perspective
  • Multi-level abstraction of motion behavior
  • Consistency and comparative analysis focus
  • Independent of implementation-specific methods

Core Principle

SENI focuses on consistency between different representations of rotational motion behavior rather than implementation-specific methods.

It provides a structured evaluation layer for analyzing system-level consistency across industrial and mobility systems.

Important Note

SENI is a conceptual framework for evaluation and analysis.

It does not define or rely on specific hardware configurations, sensor implementations, or algorithmic processing methods.
