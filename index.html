SENI

Wheel-Level Motion Intelligence for Mobility System Evaluation and Validation

Enabling additional wheel-level motion observability for ADAS validation and autonomous mobility evaluation.

Purpose

SENI is a conceptual framework designed to enhance motion observability in mobility systems by enabling additional wheel-level motion insights under real-world operating conditions.

System Context

Modern mobility systems rely on model-based estimation of vehicle motion states. In real-world conditions, discrepancies can emerge due to changing road surfaces, vehicle dynamics, sensor limitations, and operational environments.

Concept Overview
SENI provides a system-level evaluation layer for analyzing consistency between estimated and observed motion behavior while supporting validation workflows without requiring disclosure of proprietary control algorithms.
Intended Applications
  • Vehicle dynamics evaluation and benchmarking
  • ADAS validation workflows
  • Autonomous mobility development environments
  • Motion estimation model assessment
  • Real-world versus estimated behavior analysis
Value Proposition
  • Enhanced motion observability at the wheel level
  • Improved understanding of system behavior under real-world conditions
  • Structured comparison between estimated and observed motion states
  • Support for validation and verification workflows
  • Potential reduction of development and testing uncertainty
Status

Concept-stage framework for research, evaluation, and industry discussion purposes.
